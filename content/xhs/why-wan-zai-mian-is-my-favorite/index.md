# Why wanzaimian (重庆小面) is my favorite food in China

I really love those noodles! If I had the option, I would eat them every day of
the week. Unfortunately here in Singapore I haven’t found any really good
xiaomian yet. The best ones I had were in Chongqing (of course) and in Shenzhen.

Why do I love it so much?

Us laowai hate eating stuff with bones, really. It took me so long to get used
to eating meat with bones inside it. In Western culture it’s really not the
norm.

Second, we don’t really like wet noodles. I always feel like when we eat noodles
with broth, all the flavor stays in the soup and nothing sticks to the noodles.

Here wanzaimian comes to save the day! You have minced meat so you don’t need to
worry about bones, and the mala sauce together with the noodle structure makes
everything stick together into one perfect bite!

I remember the first time I tried it. I saw the texture of the beans you put
inside and it really reminded me of one of my favorite dishes back in my home
country - hummus! This really made me fall in love with wanzaimian.

The picture I posted is my only picture with wanzaimian (because I always finish
it so fast). It’s me enjoying the noodles in the wild streets of Chongqing.
