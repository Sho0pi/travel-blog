# Climbing Emeishan


# Part 1

I climbed Emeishan in one day. That was the hardest thing I ever did in my life.
I was frustrated, my mental state was not the best. I wanted to quit so many
times throughout the hike, but in the end I did it! And I proved to myself that
I can do anything!

When I was traveling in Sichuan, I saw that there is this famous hike to
Emeishan. I had a couple days to travel alone before meeting my girlfriend, so I
decided to do it.

I am a fairly experienced hiker, so I thought it wouldn’t be a problem. I bought
a train ticket to Emeishan for the next few days and started preparing. Little
did I know I would almost want to quit the hike even before starting it.

The next day me and my girlfriend had a huge fight. We were long-distance and
were not next to each other, so it was really hard to put this fight behind us.
I kept thinking about it all day - thinking about what I was going to say and
how I was going to respond to her when we met. It made me feel drained, and like
I was a loser and couldn’t achieve anything.

I really need to work on that emotion in me - but not today. Maybe one day.

I started to become scared of climbing Emeishan. I read online that there are
snowstorms sometimes at the top, and there are monkeys that steal your stuff. I
was in a bad mindset, and I didn’t have my girlfriend or any friend to tell me
"You can do it, you got this!"

Suddenly this challenge, that a few days ago I was like "It’s the easiest
challenge ever", became this huge thing that I needed to overcome without love
and support from my loved ones. I felt alone. I just wanted to quit, be sick, or
come up with some excuse not to do it.

Just a heads up - I kept talking to my girlfriend all the time, but because we
didn’t put this fight behind us, I couldn’t come to peace with it and I felt
like she was not really supporting me 100%.

I took the train and kept battling with myself, thinking "I’m not feeling well,
maybe I should rest tomorrow", and when I arrived in the town, I realized I was
not prepared at all!! My hiking gear was: running shoes from Taobao, a leather
jacket, and my work bag.

This really didn’t help the situation of me feeling lost and unsupported. Like
it’s not enough, when I went to the store to buy food and a walking stick, the
auntie showed me a photo from the peak taken yesterday. It was covered in snow
and you couldn’t even see the pathway.

When I told her I was going to climb the mountain tomorrow, she said it was too
dangerous and called me crazy!

I was devastated. I wanted to quit so badly. I decided I was quitting, so I
called my girlfriend to tell her the news, and thank god I called her, because
she said: "I know I’m mad at you right now, but I support you. I know you can do
it. Don’t listen to the auntie, believe in yourself. I believe in you!"

I told her goodnight, packed my bag, turned off the light, and went to sleep,
preparing to wake up at 5am to start the hike tomorrow!
