# Why you have to go see horse racing while you are in Hong Kong

When I was living in Hong Kong, I had a chance to experience horse racing in
Happy Valley, and to be honest, that was one of my best memories from the city.

Before I start, don’t be like "Yeah I live in Hong Kong, I will go check it out
one day..." We both know you won’t. Read this and hopefully it will convince
you.

Horse racing in Happy Valley happens every Wednesday! (except during summer).
The races actually happen during evening-night time, making it one of the best
attractions to do after a hard day of work.

And what is my favorite thing about it? You can drink beer and bet!

Betting actually starts from a very small amount (20 HKD), so usually we would
set a small budget (200 HKD) and slowly spend it throughout the night. I don’t
really encourage betting - we were doing it for the experience hahahah (and I
never won).

What I really loved though was the atmosphere there.

You see the older generation of Hong Kong standing around with newspapers,
studying numbers, doing calculations, discussing horses like their life depends
on it. It makes you realize how deeply horse racing is connected to the culture
of the city.

The environment becomes really thrilling once the race is about to start. People
slowly gather near the track. Everyone is waiting. Then suddenly the horses fly
past you at full speed. You really start to admire those animals.

That was probably the craziest part for me.

You can stand right next to the track and literally feel the wind from the
horses when they pass by. The ground shakes a little, people start screaming,
and for a few seconds the entire place feels electric.

I posted pictures here of us having the time of our lives at the racing track.
Race night is really one of my favorite nights in Hong Kong.

For drinking, there are bars inside where you can grab beer. Or “DON’T TELL
ANYONE” just buy beers from 7-Eleven hide them in your work bag.

There are multiple races throughout the night so the flow is basically:

Bet → Race → Break → Repeat.

The entrance uses the Octopus card actually (makes you realize how deeply it’s
baked into Hong Kong culture).

You can come anytime and leave anytime during the event.

- address: 跑馬地馬場
- How to get there: You can take the old tram right to Happy Valley, then it’s a
  short 5-minute walk to the main entrance.

For more information you can look at the Happy Valley official site.
