Why I Left Hong Kong

2026-05-09 
About 500 words 2 minutes 
- views  

Why I left Hong Kong:

I lived for about 1 year in Hong Kong. This was an amazing yet very chaotic experience. I met my girlfriend there while traveling and decided to stay with her (she lived there for about 3 years).

Hong Kong was really too much for us.

Don’t get me wrong. I think Hong Kong is an amazing travel destination and you should definitely go there. The scenery is amazing and the food is so good.

It’s just not a place to live and build a life in.

Hong Kong people - at least most of them - are rude, not really nice, and too self-centered. Every time I would walk on a crowded street or step into a crowded metro, I felt like I was fighting with everyone for space and to be seen.

Our space in Hong Kong was tiny! (less than 40 sqm). It is really hard to live life in this small “shoe box”.

After some time, I felt Hong Kong got the best of me and made me become a person I don’t want to be. I started to be less nice to people around me, less patient, and got stressed about small things easily. It was really too hard, so we decided to leave.

We just moved to Singapore and so far the experience is very different. The pathways are wide enough so you don’t need to push anyone (hahahah). Our house is much bigger and nicer, there is a lot of greenery around us, and we are just around the corner from a river and a park, so we don’t need to go on a hike to escape the city (like HK). Most importantly, we feel like the work-life balance, while not as good as Europe, is much better and healthier than Hong Kong.

I posted pictures here of us having fun in Hong Kong. I know I spread some hate about Hong Kong here, but we really had some great times and memories there. This place will always have a warm place in my heart. Hopefully one day I can come back as a tourist, go to the horse racing, and experience the big city life.

What is your experience with Hong Kong? I want to know if people have a similar experience to what we had. And what do you think about Singapore? I would love to hear from people who have already lived here for a long time and how it compares to Hong Kong.

What do you prefer?
