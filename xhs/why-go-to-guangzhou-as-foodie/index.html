Why You Should Go to GuangZhou if You Are Foodie

Why you should go to GuangZhou if you are a foodie

When we were living in Hong Kong, we went on a weekend getaway to GuangZhou, and to be honest I kind of regret not doing it more often.

If you are a foodie, this place is like heaven on earth. I have been to many cities and provinces in China, but this city has the best food by far. And the funniest thing is we discovered a hidden-gem by accident.

We had a free weekend and didn’t really know where to go. We already traveled to ShenZhen quite often, so we decided to explore a new city. My girlfriend really likes coconut hotpot, and she heard there are very good ones in GuangZhou. Even though I don’t like it that much, I still decided to tell her “yes let’s do it” and thank god I said it, because we discovered one street with some of the best food in China.

We took a train from Hong Kong train station to GuangZhou (only 40 minutes!!!)

After we arrived in the city, we decided to take a taxi to the hotpot place. On the way, suddenly the cab slowed down and we stopped moving :( We looked outside and the street was packed with people and food stalls. Everyone was sitting on the sidewalks and eating so many different kinds of food.

We were so hungry because we didn’t eat the entire day. So we just asked the taxi driver to leave us there, and little did we know we discovered the BEST street food street!

We walked around and entered so many different stores and shops, and tried so many kinds of food.

Food we tried:

  • fried pigeon( 炸乳鸽) - the best pigeon I had in my life
  • black chicken soup (乌鸡药材汤)
  • coconut chicken soup (椰子鸡汤)- inside the coconut itself!!
  • black sesame fudge (芝麻糊)
  • premium durian
  • and much more that I already forgot

The food was so good, so instead of going for a day trip we decided to stay over in GuangZhou. That way we had a chance to fill our belly with more amazing food we hadn’t managed to eat that day.

I posted pictures here of the food. I’m sorry if the quality is not the best. The food was too good so we finished everything before having a chance to take a picture. (I left a picture of me playing ping pong with locals as an apology.)

And I know you might think “Why does this laowai know about Chinese food?” Let me tell you this: I traveled to China 14 times in the past year, all the way from HaiNan to Beijing, so if I see good food I know to stop and appreciate it. If you still don’t believe me, take a look at the comments below.

Secret street location: 司马路/西华路路口

If you have no plans this coming weekend, you must go there and give it a try! I really encourage you to go tomorrow (or on your next day off) and let me know how it goes. I hope I convinced you. Any other places you want to hear my opinion about? Do you think there are places with better food? (please don’t say Beijing - I didn’t really like the food there - but that is another story already)
