# Why I Love Chongqing and Am Thinking About Moving There

We spent 1 week in Chongqing and it was really nice, it made me think about
moving there in the future.

The only thing that killed me was my legs from all the stairs and steps I needed
to take.

I love Chongqing food. I know it's spicy, but you just ask for "Wei La" and it
will be good enough for you to eat. Some places in the city are really chaotic,
but many places are very chill as well. I loved the way the river is shaped
within the city and how the city is built on top of it.

We took a boat ride alongside the river and it was truly romantic.

I know that people say that people from Chongqing have a strong attitude, I
don't mind that. When they scream and yell on the street, it makes me feel like
they are really showing their emotions. It makes me think that they are real and
genuine! I love that.

And let me just talk about the food again! The mala hotpot was amazing, and of
course my favorite Chinese food, Chongqing xiaomian!

Let me be clear, not everything is sunshine and rainbows. You can feel that this
is the most populated city in China, there are people everywhere! Coming from
Hong Kong, that was still too much for me.

Also - I love biking! Now we are living in Singapore and we bike every day, but
in Chongqing it would simply not be possible.

I am still debating if we should ever move there, even for a short time (like
half a year). What do you think? Do you like living in Chongqing? Did I miss
anything about the city?

We are going to travel there again soon, so let me know if there are extra
things and foods I must try!
