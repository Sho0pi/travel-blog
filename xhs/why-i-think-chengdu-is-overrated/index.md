# Why I Think Chengdu Is Overrated

I spent about one week in Chengdu and I will be honest with you, I don't really
understand what the hype is about. Yeah, there are pandas and panda statues, and
they are cute, but other than that there is not much the city has to offer.

Before Chengdu, I was traveling in Chongqing. It had the best food and the most
interesting city landscape.

Then after a 2 hour train ride, I arrived in Chengdu and I felt like I was just
in another ordinary city with average food. The only thing that I actually liked
there was Chunxi Road (春熙路), the walking street that kind of reminded me of
Causeway Bay in Hong Kong or Orchard Road in Singapore.

But other than that, I felt nothing, no excitement, nothing.

I went to see pandas in the zoo, but the only thing I saw was a bunch of people
trying to take pictures. The line was so long that I just gave up. Now I watch
the pandas on the zoo live TV channel instead hahahah.

I must say, I came to Chengdu after a fight with my girlfriend, and I was alone,
maybe that's why I looked at the city in a negative way.

And I know Sichuan is amazing, there are so many things to do and see (I did
some of them as well), but Chengdu, except for being the hub to go to
attractions from, has nothing else to offer.

What do you think? Did I miss anything about Chengdu? I'm thinking about giving
it another try, but I'm scared to be disappointed again.
